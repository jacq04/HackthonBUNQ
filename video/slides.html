the four failure modes

Real groups break
on ordinary problems.

Late or missed payments

One person falls behind. No system to track it, no fair penalty — so the whole group tenses up and trust erodes.

Payment disputes

"I paid." "You didn't." No receipts, no ledger, no neutral party. It becomes personal, and it breaks friendships.

Unfair payout ordering

Whoever shouts loudest gets cycle one. Nobody considers who actually needs the money first. Resentment builds.

Mid-cycle emergencies

Someone's parent needs surgery and they can't wait three months. There's no clean exit — they disappear and take the trust with them.

§ the architecture

Three planes. One pot.
Each has one job — only one.

i. the bank
bunq
real EUR · sandbox rails

Every contribution is a bunq payment. Every payout is a bunq payment. No fake money, no hand-waving custody. Real euros, real rails.

ii. the accountant
TigerBeetle
double-entry · atomic · invariant

Six accounts per pod. Two-phase transfers. debits ≤ credits — the pool cannot owe more than it holds. Math, not trust.

iii. the organizer
Claude × 6
Sonnet 4.6 · Haiku 4.5 · tool-use

Six agents, six narrow jobs. They draft, remind, mediate, compute exits, order payouts. They propose. You approve — with your face.
